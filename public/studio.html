About the studio.

Every piece begins with intention—shaped by hand, refined through slow making, and finished with care. Tali's approach honors the natural rhythm of clay, allowing form and function to emerge through patient observation rather than rushed production. The studio is a living workspace where tools, shelves, and the kiln corner tell the story of daily practice.

This is a place where creativity unfolds quietly, where imperfections are celebrated, and where each vessel carries the mark of the hands that shaped it. The work is honest, unhurried, and rooted in the belief that beautiful objects deserve time to become what they are meant to be.

From clay to kiln

Wedging & throwing

The process begins with wedging—kneading the clay to remove air bubbles and create an even consistency. Then, at the wheel, I center the clay and slowly coax it upward, feeling the walls thin and rise beneath my palms. Each piece starts with this intimate dance between hand and material.

Trimming

Once leather-hard, each piece returns to the wheel for trimming. With a sharp ribbon tool, I carve away excess clay from the foot, refining the form and revealing the final silhouette. This stage is about precision and rhythm—listening to the clay as it curls away in delicate ribbons.

Glazing tests

Before committing to a full glaze, I test each recipe on small tiles—layering, brushing, dipping. The chemistry of glaze is both science and intuition. I document every combination, watching how colors shift and surfaces develop, building a personal library of textures and finishes unique to my studio.

Firing & finishing

The final transformation happens in the kiln. Bisque firing hardens the clay, then glaze firing brings the colors to life at temperatures exceeding 1200°C. When I open the kiln door days later, there's always a moment of anticipation—the reveal of how heat and time have alchemized raw materials into finished art.

What guides the work

Authenticity

Every piece carries the marks of the hand that shaped it. No molds, no mass production—just clay, time, and intention. The imperfections aren't flaws; they're proof of a real person at work.

Calm collecting

There's no urgency here, no countdown timers or limited-time pressure. The pre-order model creates space for thoughtful choices and personal connection, letting you collect pieces that truly resonate rather than impulse-buy.

Craft over rush

Each piece takes the time it needs. Rushing clay means compromising quality, and quality is the only thing worth preserving. Slow work creates objects that last, both physically and emotionally.

Making fewer, better pieces means meeting collectors as people, not customers—and that human exchange is what makes this work meaningful.

Studio notes

Each piece carries the unpredictable beauty of fire and time. Working with clay means embracing the unknown—glazes shift, temperatures vary, and every kiln opening reveals something new. These reflections capture moments from the studio, where patience and experimentation shape every creation.

"The glaze never behaves exactly as planned. Sometimes a piece emerges with unexpected depth, a color I didn't anticipate. Those surprises remind me why I love this work—the kiln has its own voice, and I've learned to listen."

"Kiln days are the quietest days. While the fire does its work, I sit with sketches and tea, planning the next batch. There's a calm in waiting, in trusting the process. When the door finally opens, it feels like unwrapping a gift I made for myself."
