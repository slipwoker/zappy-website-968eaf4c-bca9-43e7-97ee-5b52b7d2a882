Works

Each piece begins as raw clay shaped by hand—cups that nestle in your palm, bowls with subtle asymmetry, vases with surfaces that shift in the light. The glazes develop depth through layering and firing, creating tactile edges and unexpected color variations that make every vessel unique.

All ceramics are handmade in small batches. Slight variations in form, glaze, and finish are natural characteristics of the process. New works become available through periodic pre-order windows, with pickup scheduled at the studio.

Seasonal Variations

Each piece evolves with the seasons—spring's lighter glazes give way to autumn's deeper earth tones. Glaze runs are never quite the same twice; they pool and break differently depending on humidity, kiln placement, and the clay's mood that day. Forms shift too, as I explore new curves and proportions, letting each collection reflect where I am in my practice.

Materials & surfaces

Clay bodies

Each piece begins with stoneware or porcelain chosen for its tone and strength. The clay's natural texture becomes part of the final character—some surfaces stay raw and sandy, others are smoothed to a soft finish. Every batch is tested for consistency, ensuring pieces can withstand daily use while retaining their handmade warmth.

Glaze approach

Glazes are layered in thin coats, each firing revealing subtle shifts in color and depth. Test tiles guide the process—small experiments that inform larger pieces. The studio palette leans toward earthy neutrals with occasional flashes of copper or iron, all developed through careful observation and iteration rather than formulas alone.

Care notes

These pieces are made for everyday moments. Hand wash with gentle soap to preserve the glaze's soft sheen. Avoid sudden temperature changes—no freezer to oven transitions. Over time, fine utensil marks may appear; they're part of the piece's story, not flaws. Each item is dishwasher-safe but hand washing extends its life and beauty.

How collecting works

Pre-order announcement

Each collection begins with an announcement shared through email and social media. You'll see photos of the new pieces, learn about the inspiration behind them, and find out when the making window opens. This is your invitation to reserve something before it's created.

Making window

During the making window, I craft each piece by hand in my studio. This process takes time—shaping, drying, firing, glazing, and firing again. You'll receive updates along the way, so you can follow your piece from wet clay to finished ceramic. It's a slow, intentional process that honors the material.

Pickup fair experience

When the collection is ready, I host a pickup fair at the studio. This is where you meet your piece in person, handle it, and take it home. It's also a chance to meet me, see the workspace, and connect with other collectors. The fair feels like a gathering of friends rather than a transaction.

Aftercare & staying in touch

After you take your piece home, I'm here for any questions about care or use. Many collectors stay connected through the newsletter, sharing how they use their ceramics in daily life. It's a relationship that continues beyond the pickup fair, building a community around handmade objects and thoughtful living.
